Collisions involving drunk driving in NYC


Our goal is to help NYPD locate drunk drivers in terms of time and place to be able to prevent accidents.
We did that by analyzing historical data of vehicle collision in NYC's boroughs.


  • On the first chart the trend of number of collisions involving drunk drivers can be seen.
  • First on a yearly basis for the whole period and then on a year-till-date base.
  • In 2016 the number is a intense rise compared to previous years.


  • On the second chart we took a look at the whole available period in terms of months in each borough.
  • Our goal was to identify patterns occuring throughout different districts.
  • Using the buttons below, each borough can be observed.

  • After analyzing each area, we took a look at each month to see which seem to be the most probable for collisions involving drunk drivers.
  • The chart clearly shows us that the main season for this kind of accident are the summer months.
  • Interesting to see that the winter months underperform. We expected to have higher number there due to the holidays.

  • In terms of hour-of-day the following chart helps us to highlight peek periods
  • Night hours take a leap ahead of others. The heaviest hours are between 12 pm and 5 am
  • To be able to handle the greater load, we identified the major locations in terms of Zipcode areas.
  • To be able to rearrange units we collected the 40 most and least busy area.


Our final recommendation is to redirect forces from less busy locations to the heavily impacted areas in rush hours in order to prevent drunk drivers to cause collisions and accidents.
